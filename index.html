The preference for beef/buffalo meat among many people in Kerala can be attributed to various cultural, historical, and socioeconomic factors.


Culinary Traditions

kerala has a rich culinary heritage that includes a variety of meat dishes. Buffalo is commonly used in traditional reciepes, such as bufalo curry, fry, pickles, and smoked idiyirachi etc., reflecting the state's diverse food culture.

Religious Diversity

Kerala is known for it's religious plurality, with significant populations of Christians and muslims, both of whom traditionally consume beef/buffalo meat. This contrasts with some other region s of india, where hindi dietary practices may limit buffalo meat consumption.

Economics Factors

For many communities in Kerala, cattle rearing and buffalo production are economically significant, buffalo is often more affordable than other meats., making it a staple protein source for many households.

Social and Cultural Identity

In some communities, consuming beef/buffalo meat is part of their cultural identity and practice. It is often featured in communal meals and celebrations, reinforcing social bonds.



Overall, the love for beef/buffalo meat in Kerala is deeply intertwined with the state's cultural, social, and economic fabric, making it an integral part of many people's lives.

Discover the Benefits of Bestome's Smoked Buffalo Meat

1. Is Beef/Buffalo meat good for health?

Beef/bufalo meat is an excellent source of iron. The iron in beef/bufalo meat helps our body produce hemoglobin, a protein that helps our blood carry oxygen from our lungs to the rest of our body. Not consuming enough iron defence anemia, meaning our body isn't getting enough oxygen. The bufalo meat is dried slowly by the smoke from the fire wood which turnsit into a deep brown colour but keeps the insides looking red and fresh. Different dishes can be made from this dried bufalo meat. The salty, sweet, spicy tastes combining with the smell of the smoke are unique. Especially smoke is a natural antioxidant good for our health.

2. What are the specialties of "Bestome" smoked buffalo meat and Idiyirachi?

Smoked buffalo meat Idiyirachi is an exceptional natural dish. Creating smoked buffalo meat is a time-consuming process that requires patience. It begins with taking a non-fatted piece of buffalo meat, typically from the shoulder, neck, or back, and cutting it into very thin, small pieces. The meat is then seasoned with salt, turmeric powder, pepper powder, and traditional spicy masalas for marination. After marinating for a few hours, the meat slices are skewered onto bamboo sticks, which are then hung in the kitchen or smoke chamber, giving it its distinctive name.

3. What are the key nutritional benefits of “Bestome” smoked buffalo meat compared to other types of red meat?

Smoked buffalo meat offers a rich source of high-quality protein with less fat content compared to other red meats, making it a leaner choice that still provides robust flavor. The smoking process enhances its taste without the need for additional oils or fats, keeping it relatively low in calories. Buffalo meat is also rich in essential nutrients like iron, zinc, and B vitamins, especially B12, which supports energy levels and cognitive function. Compared to other red meats, smoked buffalo meat is typically lower in cholesterol, making it a heart-friendly option while still delivering a satisfying, smoky flavor profile.
